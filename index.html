Key Points About Digital Fluorescent Colors:

Location: Fluorescent colors appear at the outer edge of HSV color wheels where saturation reaches 100%. They don't have a dedicated "section" but rather occupy the perimeter.

RGB Pattern: Most fluorescent colors have extreme RGB values - often with one or more channels at 255 (maximum) and others at 0 (minimum), creating the high contrast that makes them appear to "glow."

Hue Ranges: The most common fluorescent colors fall in specific hue ranges: • 300-330° (hot pinks, magentas) • 15-30° (safety orange, electric orange) • 60-90° (lime green, chartreuse) • 180-210° (electric blues, cyans)

Digital Limitation: Real fluorescent materials emit more light than they receive by converting UV to visible light. Computer screens can only approximate this effect through high saturation and strategic color combinations.
